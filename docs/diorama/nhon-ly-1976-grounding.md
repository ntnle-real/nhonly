# Historical Grounding Document: The Boy on the Beach, Nhơn Lý, 1976

## 1. What This Memory Needs Explained

This document provides historical and cultural context for the diorama "The Boy on the Beach, Nhơn Lý, 1976." It explains the significance of the scene’s elements—women with fish, basket boats, houses close to the water, and the shoreline as a workplace—to an audience unfamiliar with central Vietnamese fishing villages.

## 2. Public Historical/Contextual Grounding

### Nhơn Lý as a Fishing Village

Nhơn Lý is a coastal fishing village in Quy Nhơn city, Bình Định province, with a history dating back over 200 years. The village is known for its unique architecture, with houses built on rolling hills and pristine white sand. The name "Nhơn Lý" symbolizes peace and harmony between the people and the sea, reflecting the deep connection the villagers have with their maritime livelihood. The village has been recognized for its cultural traditions, particularly the Xương Lý fishing festival, which was officially recognized as a national intangible cultural heritage in 2025. This festival, with its unique rituals, highlights the spiritual life and maritime culture of the local fishing community, which has been closely associated with the sea for generations. [Source 1](#sources) [Source 2](#sources)

### Fishing as a Way of Life

Fishing is the primary economic activity in Nhơn Lý, as it is in many central Vietnamese coastal villages. Small-scale fishing households are prevalent, with most operating on a very small scale. Fishing and aquaculture contribute significantly to the income of fisher households in the region, with an average of 75% of household income derived from these activities. The South Central Coast has high levels of household involvement in fisheries, with 9.9% of households identifying fisheries as their main business and 11.3% as their primary employment. [Source 3](#sources)

## 3. Nhơn Lý-Specific Local Grounding

### The Rhythm of the Fishing Village

The daily life in Nhơn Lý revolves around the fishing rhythm. Early mornings are the liveliest when boats dock at the village with various kinds of fish and squid caught. Women are often responsible for sorting, cleaning, and laying fish out to dry, as well as selling the catch at local markets or to traders. This work is crucial for preserving the quality and value of the seafood and directly supports the household income. [Source 4](#sources)

### The Role of Women

Women in Nhơn Lý, as in many Vietnamese fishing villages, play a vital role in the local economy and community life. While men typically work on the boats and handle the actual fishing, women are deeply involved in essential onshore activities such as processing and selling fish, making and mending nets, and managing homestays for tourists. Women’s Unions and other local organizations often play a key role in community decision-making, rights allocation, and information sharing. [Source 5](#sources)

## 4. Object-by-Object Meaning

### Thúng Chai (Basket Boat)

The thúng chai, or "basket boat," is a unique and iconic Vietnamese watercraft with a fascinating history. Most sources agree that the thúng chai originated during the French colonial era in Vietnam. When the French imposed high taxes on boat ownership, resourceful local fishermen created these round, bamboo-woven boats. They argued that since the thúng chai were technically "baskets" rather than boats, they should not be subject to taxation. This clever workaround allowed poor fishermen to continue their livelihoods without paying the prohibitive taxes. The boats are traditionally made by weaving bamboo strips into a circular frame, then coating the structure with waterproof resin made from coconut oil, tar, or sometimes fibreglass. With proper maintenance, a thúng chai can last for decades. The design is not only tax-efficient but also highly functional, allowing fishermen to navigate shallow waters and transport goods or people with ease. [Source 6](#sources)

### Fish Baskets and Fish Trade

Fish baskets are essential tools in the daily life of Nhơn Lý. They are used to transport fish from the boats to the shore and to the market. The fish trade is a significant part of the local economy, with women playing a crucial role in the selection, bargaining, and selling of fish. The peak time of fish catching in Nhơn Lý is in the summer, with aqua products mainly consisting of tuna, scad, and squid. [Source 7](#sources)

### Women Beside Baskets of Fish

Women in Nhơn Lý are often seen beside baskets of fish, reflecting their central role in the fish trade. They are responsible for selecting the best fish, bargaining with buyers, and ensuring that the fish are properly cleaned and prepared for sale. This scene is a common sight in the early mornings when the fishing boats return to shore. [Source 8](#sources)

### Houses Close to the Water

Houses in Nhơn Lý are built close to the water, reflecting the villagers' deep connection to the sea. This proximity allows for easy access to the fishing boats and the daily activities centered around the shore. The architecture of the houses, with low roofs and stone fences, is adapted to the coastal environment and the needs of the fishing community. [Source 9](#sources)

### Shoreline as Workplace

The shoreline in Nhơn Lý is not just a scenic backdrop but a bustling workplace. It is where the fishing boats dock, where fish are unloaded and sorted, and where the daily trade and activities of the village take place. The shoreline is the heart of the village’s economic and social life, reflecting the integral role of the sea in the community. [Source 10](#sources)

## 5. What Remains Oral Memory Rather Than Public History

While much of the daily life and economic activities in Nhơn Lý are well-documented, certain aspects of the village’s history and culture remain part of oral memory. These include personal stories of the fishermen and their families, the specific rituals and traditions passed down through generations, and the individual experiences of the villagers. These oral memories are crucial for understanding the full context of the diorama and the lives of the people it represents.
